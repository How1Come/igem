Abstract :

Our team aims to produce two different types of engineered proteins to target lung cancer cells and check for whether two groups of peptides have any synergic effects with doxorubicin.

Lung cancer was the commonest cancer in Hong Kong. It accounted for 15.5% of new cancer cases in 2021 in Hong Kong. Galectin is a common receptor of lung cancer cell.

We used AI-algorithm to generate peptide sequences by targeting galectin receptor on lung cancer cell membrane.

Moreover, 4 peptides from Cordyceps militaris, a well‚Äêknown dietary therapy in anticancer treatment, have been selected to test their effectiveness to lung cancer.

Then, those peptides have been expressed by BL21. Finally, A549, a lung cancer cell line, has been used to test the effectiveness of those peptides and check for whether two groups of peptides have any synergic effects with doxorubicin, a common chemotherapy medication used to treat cancer.

If there are synergic effects, lower concentration of doxorubicin can be used, so that it can reduce the side effects of it.
